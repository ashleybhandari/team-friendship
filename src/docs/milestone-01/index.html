Project Overview

In 2023, UMass announced that it would no longer be able to guarantee on-campus housing for all undergraduates who requested it. The room selection process ended early, leaving 900 students without housing for the fall. These students rushed to the UMass Off Campus Housing website, Facebook Marketplace, Instagram, Discord, and more to blindly message usernames in hopes of coming upon compatible housing and roommates. KeyMate was born to ease this effort by centralizing the search process and presenting students with important data upfront. Akin to a dating app, each user creates a profile in which they describe their traits, habits, and preferences surrounding roommates and housing. KeyMate uses this information to tailor each user's feed. This way, users can tell whether they like each other at a glance; if two users are compatible and interested in each other, they can then safely message within the web application before exchanging contact information. Our team hopes that this candid and holistic approach to finding potential roommates and housing will help mitigate the housing crisis and relieve students' stress. The scope of the project is currently limited to UMass Amherst, but should it find success, we hope to expand it to other schools.

Application Highlights

One critical component of our web application is providing users with the ability to tailor their profiles to accurately reflect their preferences and requirements for housing and roommates. This feature enables students to input specific criteria, such as budget, location preferences, lifestyle habits, and personality traits, to match them with suitable housing options and potential roommates. With this feature, we ensure that they receive personalized recommendations that align with their unique needs and preferences. This not only streamlines the search process but also increases the likelihood of finding an ideal housing situation that meets their expectations.

The second component is a feed that is filtered based on one's preferences. A filtering system curates an individual's feed based on their specified roommate and housing preferences. This functionality ensures that users are presented with listings and roommate profiles that closely match their desired criteria, thereby saving time and effort in sorting through irrelevant options. Users can apply filters such as location, budget, move-in/move-out dates, listing attributes, housing amenities, and roommate preferences to narrow down their options. The filtering logic would then process these user-inputted parameters and match them against the attributes and details of the available listings and roommate profiles in the database.

The last critical feature is a messaging system for communication. Effective communication is essential for facilitating connections between students seeking housing and roommates. Therefore, we have integrated a messaging system into our application that allows users to safely express their interest in potential roommates or housing listings and initiate further contact. This feature allows seamless communication between roommates, allowing them to discuss details, arrange viewings, and establish compatibility before making any commitments. Additionally, the messaging system incorporates safety measures like reporting and blocking functionalities to ensure a secure and respectful environment for all users.

Real-World Connection

KeyMate was created to support students as they navigate the ongoing housing crisis at UMass Amherst. We recognized that finding housing accommodations has become an annual stressor for many, including ourselves: one must scour social media in search of an open bed that ticks their boxes, have conversations with a myriad of strangers, and hope that their personality and lifestyle will help them get chosen over other candidates. It is a long and frustrating competition. KeyMate aims to transform this process by simplifying how people find each other, thereby expediting the overall housing search.

The housing crisis has been years in the making: over-admission has caused a massive increase in UMass's Amherst's undergraduate population, but construction of on-campus housing has not been able to keep pace. In 2023, the university announced that, due to a lack of space, it could not guarantee housing accommodations to all students who requested them. With this minimal notice, over 900 students suddenly found themselves facing housing insecurity. Around 100 students soon pitched their tents in the middle of campus for a sleep-in protest. They made the news speaking of their experiences, noting the shortage of open beds on campus and high rental costs off campus. Protests continue today, demonstrating the pervasiveness of the issue.

The inevitable consequence of the university's inaction is competition: there is high demand and low supply both on and off campus. Students race the clock to secure a dorm room. When the dorms fill up, the rest of the student body is forced to compete in Amherst's limited housing market. Rental costs rise in response, pushing many into neighboring towns. A web application cannot fix the underlying, deep-rooted problem, but it can mitigate the resulting stressors.

Students use a large variety of websites and social media applications in search of open rooms. Most advertisements are informal (such as those posted on Facebook Marketplace or Discord), creating two time-consuming issues. First, there is no system to filter listings according to one's preferences, so one must keep track of listings in which they aren't interested and remember to scroll past them. Second, the creation of listings is not guided, so descriptions of the accommodation and the people living in it are often lacking. Thus, for every listing with potential, one must have a text conversation with the poster just to fill in the gaps and decide whether they are actually interested. Depending on how quickly each party replies, a single conversation can take days to reach a conclusion. KeyMate solves both of these issues by guiding the creation of each listing, thereby ensuring that users include the important information. The application optimizes the user's time by displaying these listings in a feed: the user can filter the feed to their liking, save specific listings, and mark other listings as "Not Interested."

KeyMate's goal is to support students. Students already struggle to balance UMass's academically rigorous curriculum with their work, hobbies, and social lives. The university's inaction has added housing insecurity to this struggle, disrupting the balance. Many don't have the capacity to both navigate their regular responsibilities and search for the perfect housing—we believe that they shouldn't need this capacity. By centralizing and standardizing how students find roommates and housing (both on and off campus), KeyMate limits undue stress, keeping students focused on what matters.

Integrative Experience

The development of our web application for helping students find housing and roommates ties closely into the integrative experience requirements of our coursework and personal experiences. As students who have personally grappled with the challenges of housing insecurity and roommate searches, we recognized the need for a dedicated platform to address these issues effectively. Our app serves as a one-stop shop for looking for housing and roommates because we have provided a messaging system in which the user can directly inquire with either the leasing office or the roommate in which they are interested about the accommodation, without relying on third-party websites such as social media apps or realtor websites. Our project represents the culmination of our university education and personal growth, drawing upon specific courses and life experiences that have contributed to its development.

Throughout our academic journey, courses such as CS 220 and CS 326 have provided us with essential knowledge and skills in JavaScript, Typescript, and web development. These foundational courses equipped us with the technical expertise necessary to build a robust and user-friendly web application. Additionally, CS 325 proved invaluable for wireframing and designing the user interface, ensuring a seamless and intuitive user experience.

Personal experiences shared among our team members underscore the real-world relevance and necessity of our project. For instance, one of our team members' struggle to find someone to sublet her room during breaks highlights the limitations of relying solely on personal social media accounts and current online platforms. Similarly, the inability of another member to find compatible roommates and housing emphasizes the inadequacies of current housing resources available to students.

Our experiences of navigating housing searches late in the semester and encountering difficulties due to specific housing requirements, such as finding roommates willing to accommodate an emotional support animal, further underscored the need for a dedicated platform tailored to the unique needs of each university student. Our app will work to alleviate the stress of many students who face homelessness because they don't have the time or energy to consistently connect with countless people across different platforms. These experiences inspired us to create a solution that addresses these points directly, providing students with a comprehensive and user-centric platform for finding housing and roommates.

Furthermore, our project aligns with the principles of computing for good, as emphasized in courses like CICS 305. By creating a platform that addresses the pressing needs of students tired of the arduous process of finding suitable housing and roommates, we are leveraging technology to make a positive impact on our community. The overwhelming interest in KeyMate, as expressed by fellow students, further validates the importance and relevance of our project, as well as reinforces our commitment to computing for social good.

In essence, our web application represents the convergence of our academic careers, personal experiences, and commitment to leveraging technology for the betterment of our community. By addressing the challenges of housing insecurity and roommate searches, we are not only fulfilling the requirements of our coursework, but also making a tangible difference in the lives of students.
